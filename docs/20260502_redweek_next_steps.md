## Side-realizations from this session

A few framing things that crystallized while we were doing this — worth keeping for the next time we touch the timeshare:

1. **Interval International is past tense for this household.** Membership lapsed Sep 2019, never reactivated, and the exchange model is in long-term decline industry-wide. Used II twice in 15 years (2010 Hawaii, 2016 Antigua) and life kept going fine without it. Renting on RedWeek + cash-funded vacations is the modern winning move.

2. **II vs RedWeek is not apples-to-apples.** They do different things — II is exchange (trade your week for someone else's), RedWeek is rental/sale (cash for your week). Andy's instinct that II is "lazier" is right; the model is dying because owners figured out that "give me cash, I'll book my own vacation" beats "trade for a complicated thing with stacked fees."

3. **Mom's potential trip this year is decoupled from K5133.** Renting K5133 means we can't also use it. Mom's trip is a separate cash booking. The original `redweek_koala.md` plan (rent Cancun, take family to Royal Lahaina Sep 19-26 with the proceeds + savings) is still the cleanest play if Hawaii becomes the choice.

4. **The full-service tier is genuinely full-service.** Photos, listing copy, inquiry response, contract, payment collection — all RedWeek's job. Andy's job ends at "post the listing, send the verification docs." This is meaningfully less work than the older Standard tier in the original `redweek_koala.md` plan.

5. **The dossier-as-first-shot approach is faster than chasing one document.** Instead of waiting for Jorge's reply on a specific reservation-confirmation number that may not exist for fixed-week ownership, we sent RedWeek everything they could plausibly want in one self-contained email. Worst case they reply asking for one specific thing and we send it. Saves a verification cycle if they accept.
